Regulatory Hub

The shift to quantum-resistant cryptography is not just a technical imperative, but a regulatory one. Our Regulatory Hub provides a centralized resource to understand the key standards, mandates, and guidelines shaping the post-quantum era.

Key Regulatory Bodies & Standardization Efforts

Governments and international bodies are actively working to standardize Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) algorithms and guide organizations through the transition. Staying informed about these efforts is crucial for compliance and future-proofing your digital infrastructure.

  • National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) PQC Project

    Leading the global effort to standardize quantum-resistant public-key cryptographic algorithms.

  • U.S. National Security Memorandum 10 (NSM-10)

    Directs U.S. government agencies to migrate to PQC and sets deadlines for developing quantum-resistant systems.

Global Regulatory Landscape

While NIST is a key driver, the transition to PQC is a global effort. Several other international bodies are contributing to the standardization and adoption of quantum-safe technologies.

  • ETSI - Quantum-Safe Cryptography (QSC)

    The European Telecommunications Standards Institute provides specifications and guidelines for quantum-safe solutions, focusing on interoperability and practical implementation.

  • ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 27 - IT Security Techniques

    This international committee is working on standardizing cryptographic mechanisms, including post-quantum algorithms, to ensure global compatibility.

PQC Migration Timeline: Key Milestones

The transition to post-quantum cryptography is a multi-year process. Understanding the key dates is essential for planning your organization's migration strategy.

2024

NIST publishes the first final PQC standards (ML-KEM, ML-DSA, SLH-DSA), marking the official start of the migration period.

2025 (Current Year)

Early adopters begin implementing hybrid cryptographic schemes. Focus shifts to inventorying cryptographic assets and developing crypto-agility.

2026-2028

Broader industry adoption begins. We anticipate the finalization of the second set of NIST PQC standards and updated guidance from regulatory bodies.

2030-2035

Major regulatory deadlines for migration in critical infrastructure and government sectors are expected to fall within this period. Full transition to PQC becomes a compliance necessity.

Industry-Specific Considerations

While PQC migration affects all sectors, some face more immediate regulatory pressure and unique challenges due to the long-term sensitivity of their data.

  • Finance: Financial institutions must protect data with a long shelf-life, such as mortgage and loan information. "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" is a significant threat, making early adoption of PQC a priority for protecting long-term assets and maintaining customer trust.
  • Healthcare: Patient health records (PHI) must be kept secure for decades. Regulations like HIPAA, combined with the long-term value of health data, place immense pressure on healthcare organizations to protect against future quantum threats today.
