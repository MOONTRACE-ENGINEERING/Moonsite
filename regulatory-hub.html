Regulatory Hub: Navigating Post-Quantum Compliance

The shift to quantum-resistant cryptography is not just a technical imperative, but a regulatory one. Our Regulatory Hub provides a centralized resource to understand the key standards, mandates, and guidelines shaping the post-quantum era.

Key Regulatory Bodies & Standardization Efforts

Governments and international bodies are actively working to standardize Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) algorithms and guide organizations through the transition. Staying informed about these efforts is crucial for compliance and future-proofing your digital infrastructure.

  • National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) PQC Project

    Leading the global effort to standardize quantum-resistant public-key cryptographic algorithms.

  • Agence Nationale de la Sécurité des Systèmes d'Information (ANSSI)

    France's national cybersecurity agency, providing guidance on PQC adoption.

  • European Commission Quantum Technologies Flagship

    The EU's long-term initiative to accelerate the development of quantum technologies, including quantum-safe communication.

Data Protection & Industry-Specific Regulations

The quantum threat impacts existing data protection laws and industry-specific compliance frameworks. Organizations must consider how PQC migration aligns with their obligations to protect sensitive information.

  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

    Ensuring the long-term confidentiality and integrity of personal data in the face of quantum attacks.

  • Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)

    Protecting electronic protected health information (ePHI) from future decryption by quantum computers.

  • Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)

    Maintaining secure environments for credit card data, which will require PQC updates.
