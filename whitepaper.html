2. The Problem Statement: A Strategic Imperative

Why Current Cryptographic Systems are Inadequate in a Quantum World.

The Quantum Threat to Cryptography

The widespread use of public-key cryptography (PKI), including RSA and ECC, forms the backbone of digital security. However, with Shor’s algorithm, these systems will become obsolete, jeopardizing long-term data confidentiality. This is not a future problem; it's a present risk due to the "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" threat, where adversaries are already collecting encrypted data today to decrypt with a CRQC tomorrow.

The threat of quantum computing is imminent and requires a proactive response today.

Challenges Faced by Organizations

  • Enterprises: Struggle with vast legacy systems and a lack of tools for comprehensive cryptographic inventory.
  • Governments: Must comply with mandates like NSM-10 while protecting national security secrets that require decades of confidentiality.
  • Developers: Face a lack of accessible, developer-friendly tools and a fragmented PQC ecosystem, making implementation non-trivial.

Michele Mosca's Theorem: Quantifying the Quantum Threat

The urgency of the quantum threat is best framed by Dr. Michele Mosca's Theorem, which posits that the time for data to remain secure (X) plus the time to migrate systems (Y) must be less than the time until a CRQC is operational (Z). As **(X + Y) > Z**, organizations are already running out of time.

"There is a 1 in 7 chance that some fundamental public-key crypto will be broken by quantum by 2026, and a 1 in 2 chance of the same by 2031."

4. Solution Overview: The Moontrace Platform

A unified suite of tools for end-to-end cryptographic assurance.

Moontrace VANTAGE

Crypto Posture Visibility & Asset Discovery

VANTAGE is a comprehensive scanning and analytics engine that provides CISOs and DevOps teams with a clear, real-time view of their entire cryptographic surface. It automates the discovery of cryptographic assets and identifies vulnerabilities before they can be exploited.

  • **Features:** TLS scanning, CBOM generation, and risk classification of algorithms.
  • **Who it helps:** CISOs for dashboard visibility and DevOps for proactive security.

Moontrace APX

Crypto-Agile Migration Toolkit

APX is a no-code/low-code platform that simplifies the complex process of migrating legacy systems to hybrid or pure PQC stacks. It acts as an abstraction layer, automating certificate and protocol upgrades (e.g., from TLS 1.2 to TLS 1.3 with a Kyber-based key exchange).

  • **Features:** Phased migration planning, support for NIST-standardized PQC algorithms.
  • **Who it helps:** Enterprise security teams and developers building post-quantum systems.

Moontrace VPN

Hybrid PQC-Secure VPN Solution

Our PQC-enabled VPN was developed to address the lack of off-the-shelf crypto-agile VPNs. It provides a secure tunneling solution that protects data-in-transit against "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" attacks by using hybrid key exchange and authentication.

  • **Why a new VPN:** Existing solutions are not built with crypto agility in mind and are vulnerable to future quantum attacks.
  • **Use cases:** Government and defense organizations, cross-border secure communications.

Moontrace QUBEx

Hardware-Based Quantum Key Management

QUBEx is our hardware appliance for secure key lifecycle management. It provides quantum-safe symmetric key exchange and uses Quantum Random Number Generators (QRNG) to ensure true entropy. QUBEx is designed to be FIPS-ready and is the physical backbone for mission-critical security.

  • **Key features:** Dual-tunnel architecture for data and key delivery, HSM functionality.
  • **Roadmap:** Starting as a rack-mounted appliance, with plans for edge and cloud-adapter variants.

12. Appendix

Glossary of Key Terms

  • **CBOM (Cryptographic Bill of Materials):** A complete inventory of all cryptographic components, algorithms, and dependencies used within a system.
  • **PQC (Post-Quantum Cryptography):** Algorithms designed to be secure against attacks from both classical and quantum computers.
  • **HSM (Hardware Security Module):** A physical computing device that safeguards and manages digital keys for strong authentication.
  • **TLS (Transport Layer Security):** The standard security protocol for establishing encrypted links between a web server and a browser.
  • **CRYSTALS-Kyber:** A lattice-based key encapsulation mechanism selected by NIST for standardization.
  • **Shor’s Algorithm:** A quantum algorithm that can efficiently factor large numbers, posing a threat to asymmetric cryptography like RSA.
