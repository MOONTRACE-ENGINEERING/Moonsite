National Quantum Mission (NQM)

The National Quantum Mission (NQM), approved by the Union Cabinet on April 19, 2023, is a landmark initiative with a substantial budget of ₹6,003.65 crore (approx. $750 million USD) for the period 2023-24 to 2030-31. Its primary goal is to foster scientific and industrial R&D in Quantum Technology (QT) and establish a vibrant innovation ecosystem in India.

Key objectives and targets of the NQM include:

  • Quantum Computing: Develop intermediate-scale quantum computers with 20-50 physical qubits within 3 years, 50-100 qubits within 5 years, and 50-1000 qubits within 8 years, utilizing platforms like superconducting and photonic technologies. Moontrace's VANTAGE tool can assist organizations in assessing their current cryptographic posture, identifying systems that would benefit from quantum computing advancements, and preparing for the integration of NQM-driven quantum computing capabilities.
  • Quantum Communication: Establish satellite-based quantum-secured communication over 2000 km within India and extend this for long-distance secure quantum communication with other countries. Our QUBEx appliance is designed for secure quantum key management, crucial for robust quantum communication networks envisioned by the NQM.
  • Quantum Sensing & Metrology: Design highly sensitive quantum devices, including magnetometers with high sensitivity and atomic clocks for precision timing, communication, and navigation.
  • Quantum Materials & Devices: Support the design and synthesis of quantum materials (e.g., superconductors, novel semiconductors) for advanced quantum devices. Moontrace's MTLabs R&D efforts actively contribute to the understanding and application of quantum-safe materials and devices.
  • Thematic Hubs (T-Hubs): Four T-Hubs have been established in premier academic and R&D institutions focusing on Quantum Computing, Quantum Communication, Quantum Sensing & Metrology, and Quantum Materials & Devices, fostering research, skill development, and industry collaboration. Moontrace seeks to collaborate with these hubs to accelerate the development and deployment of quantum-safe solutions.

Union Budget Allocations: The NQM's budget of ₹6,003.65 crore reflects the government's strong commitment to quantum technology. This significant investment aims to accelerate quantum-led economic growth and position India as a leading nation in QTA development. Moontrace's agile and cost-effective solutions are designed to complement these national investments, ensuring broader adoption.

Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023

The Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023 is India's first cross-sectoral law on personal data protection, emphasizing the right of individuals to protect their data and regulating how digital personal data is collected, processed, stored, and transferred. Its relevance to the quantum era is profound:

  • Data Security & Quantum Threats: The Act mandates robust data security measures. As quantum computing advances, traditional encryption methods become vulnerable. The DPDP Act implicitly drives the need for organizations to adopt quantum-resistant cryptographic solutions to safeguard sensitive data and ensure compliance. Moontrace's APX (Crypto-Agile Application Exchange) directly addresses this by enabling organizations to seamlessly integrate post-quantum cryptography, ensuring data remains secure against future quantum attacks, thereby helping them meet DPDP Act's stringent security requirements.
  • Consent & Transparency: It emphasizes informed consent for data processing. In the quantum context, this means businesses must be transparent about security protocols and potential risks, outlining their plans for adopting quantum-resistant solutions. Moontrace's commitment to affordability, transparency, and trust aligns with the DPDP Act's principles, providing clear pathways for secure data handling.
  • Data Breach Notification: The Act requires timely notification of data breaches. Quantum computing could introduce complexities to breach detection and remediation, necessitating agile responses and PQC implementation to mitigate risks. Moontrace's VANTAGE and APX solutions enhance an organization's crypto-agility, allowing for quicker adaptation and mitigation in the event of a quantum-enabled breach, supporting compliance with notification timelines.
  • Quantum-Safe Ecosystem: The DPDP Act, alongside initiatives like NQM, encourages the development of a quantum-safe ecosystem, wherein data controllers and and processors implement advanced encryption techniques that can withstand quantum attacks. Moontrace actively contributes to this ecosystem by providing quantum-safe solutions, ensuring Indian organizations are at the forefront of quantum-safe data protection.

Security & Post-Quantum Cryptography

Security is the foundation of everything we do. We employ a multi-layered defense strategy to protect our systems and your data from current and future threats, including those posed by quantum computers.

Our PQC Implementation

We are actively transitioning to quantum-resistant cryptography. Our approach includes:

  • Standardized Algorithms: We are implementing algorithms selected by leading global bodies for quantum resistance, such as ML-KEM for key exchange and ML-DSA for digital signatures.
  • Phased Transition: During the transition, we are carefully implementing new cryptographic methods, combining them with existing ones where appropriate to ensure continuous security.
  • Crypto-Agility: Our systems are designed to be crypto-agile, allowing us to quickly adapt and update cryptographic algorithms as new standards emerge and the threat landscape evolves.

Data Encryption

We protect your data at every stage:

  • Encryption in Transit: All data transmitted between you and our services is encrypted using strong protocols like TLS 1.3, with hybrid key exchange mechanisms to ensure quantum resistance.
  • Encryption at Rest: Data stored on our servers is encrypted using industry-standard AES-256 encryption.

Infrastructure Security

Our infrastructure is architected for security and resilience:
